Text-to-Speech for Low-Resource Languages:
Efficient Cross-Lingual Transfer Learning with Minimal Linguistic Expertise and Resources

Phat Do1, Matt Coler1, Jelske Dijkstra2, Esther Klabbers3
1Language, Technology and Culture Department, Campus Fryslân, University of Groningen (Leeuwarden, the Netherlands)
2Fryske Akademy/Mercator Research Centre (Leeuwarden, the Netherlands)
3ReadSpeaker (Driebergen-Rijsenburg, the Netherlands)

This is the companion webpage for our paper about extensively validating our newly-proposed strategies in cross-lingual transfer learning for text-to-speech in low-resource languages: the PHOIBLE-based phoneme mapping method and the Angular Similarity of Phoneme Frequencies (ASPF) as a source-language selection criterion.

Below we present all the audio samples synthesized in our experiment: 1) Utterances in the 6 low-resource languages (Bulgarian, Georgian, Kazakh, Swahili, Urdu, and Uzbek) synthesized in the experiments, and 2) source language sentences (from the pre-trained models that were used for transfer learning) in American English, Finnish, Hindi, Japanese, and Russian.

1. Low-resource languages

For each language, we synthesized a total of 100 utterances, which were guaranteed to have a phoneme distribution as close as possible to that languages' training data (10 minutes, 160 to 200 utterances), and used them for evaluation. To avoid cluttering the page, here we only include 10 randomly-selected utterances as examples for each language.

  • Text - Orthographic text of the utterance
  • Ground-truth - Human recording of the utterance
  • English (en-US) - Source language (language of the pre-trained model)
  • Nomap - Sample from model fine-tuned without phoneme mapping
  • Map - Sample from model fine-tuned with phoneme mapping
